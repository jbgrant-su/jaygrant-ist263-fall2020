About Me

Bio

My name is Jay Grant, and I'm a Television, Radio, and Film student in the SI Newhouse School of Public Communications at Syracuse University, with a minor in Information Management and Technology. I grew up near Burlington, Vermont. I've had a passion for filmmaking since I was young, and began shooting movies with my friends from the time I was in elementary school. I began to take it seriously when I took my first movie production class as a freshman in high school. Ever since then I've been learning as much as I can about the filmmaking process, and creating as much as I can along the way.

Focuses

While I've dabbled in all elements of the filmmaking process, my primary focuses are cinematography and directing. I also enjoy screenwriting and post production. I've never had any formal training in photography, but I've been able to get by with transferring my cinematography skills, as well as learning on the fly and practicing a hell of a lot. My formal training includes directing, cinematography, color grading, and 4K workflow.

Style

I don't know that I necessarily have a signature style yet, and my preferences are constantly changing, but most of my work can be distinguished by having low saturation, muted color tones, low contrast, unconventional framing, an abundance of complimentary colors, and an emphasis on visuals and sound design as opposed to dialogue. While these have remained relatively consistant throughout my body of work, I anticipate that they will change over time.
